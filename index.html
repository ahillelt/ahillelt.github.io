Research & Teaching

I teach graduate courses in cybersecurity and computer networking, mentor students through the OSIRIS Lab, and conduct research examining how technical systems interact with human decision-making and governance structures. My interdisciplinary work combines technical expertise with conflict resolution methodologies to address AI alignment challenges and develop frameworks for responsible AI deployment.

Teaching Philosophy

Classes are project-driven and interdisciplinary, bridging theory with real-world cybersecurity challenges. Security education must be experiential and hands-on. Students learn best by breaking systems, building defenses, and understanding the socio-technical context in which technology operates.

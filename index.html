About

At the intersection of academic rigor and industry impact

Academic Mission

As an Industry Assistant Professor at NYU Tandon School of Engineering, I teach graduate and undergraduate courses in cybersecurity, mentor students through the OSIRIS Lab, and conduct research on hardware security, cryptographic systems, and AI safety. My approach combines theoretical foundations with hands-on, practical applications that prepare students for real-world challenges.

Teaching Philosophy

Security education must be experiential. Students learn by breaking things, building defenses, and understanding the socio-technical context in which systems operate.

Industry Experience

Before academia, I co-founded RocketHub, a crowdfunding platform that pioneered equity crowdfunding and was acquired in 2015. I testified before the U.S. House of Representatives on the JOBS Act and crowdfunding regulation. As Managing Partner at Stacked Capital, I invested in deep-tech ventures at the frontier of hardware, AI, and infrastructure resilience.

Consulting & Advisory

I work with organizations on security architecture, threat modeling, applied cryptography, and building security-aware engineering cultures.

Research & Publications

Applied security research with real-world impact

Physical & Hardware Security

Fault injection attacks, side-channel analysis, hardware trojans, and practical defenses for embedded systems and IoT devices. Focus on making security feasible in resource-constrained environments.

Applied Cryptography

Misuse-resistant cryptographic APIs, secure protocol design, and making strong crypto accessible to developers. Emphasis on usable security and secure-by-default systems.

AI Safety & Human-AI Interaction

Operational safety in AI systems, capability overhangs, adversarial robustness, and designing AI decision-support tools that align with human values and institutional constraints.

Critical Infrastructure Resilience

Failure modes in complex socio-technical systems, graceful degradation under attack, and recovery strategies for essential services.

Security Governance

Policy frameworks, standards development, and incentive structures that encourage responsible innovation and security investment.

Security Education at Scale

Curriculum design, hands-on lab development, and making advanced security concepts accessible to working professionals and undergraduate students.

Teaching

Hands-on cybersecurity education for the next generation

CS-GY 6843

Application Security

~45 students
Core Masters CAE/NSA Graduate Hands-on Labs

Deep dive into secure software development, common vulnerabilities (OWASP Top 10), exploit mitigation techniques, security testing, and building security into the SDLC. Students complete capture-the-flag challenges and build secure applications from scratch.

Key Topics

  • Secure coding practices and defensive programming
  • Web application vulnerabilities (XSS, CSRF, SQLi, SSRF)
  • Authentication & authorization systems
  • Cryptographic implementation and API security
  • Security testing and static/dynamic analysis
CS-GY 6903

Computer Networking

~60 students
Graduate Hands-on Labs

Comprehensive coverage of network protocols, architectures, and security from physical to application layer. Emphasis on practical implementation, protocol analysis, and network security fundamentals.

Key Topics

  • TCP/IP stack and protocol analysis
  • Routing algorithms and network architecture
  • Network security and encryption (TLS, VPNs)
  • Software-defined networking and modern infrastructure
  • Performance analysis and troubleshooting
